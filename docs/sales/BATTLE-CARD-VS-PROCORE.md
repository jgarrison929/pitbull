## Where We Win

- **All-in-one.** No more duct-taping Procore to Vista. One platform handles the full construction lifecycle from bid to close-out to final billing.
- **Cost.** A 50-person GC pays ~$59K/year for everything vs $110-160K for Procore + a financial system.
- **AI is native, not bolted on.** When a PM logs an RFI, the AI already knows the cost code, the budget impact, and which sub should respond. It sees the whole picture because it IS the whole picture.
- **Self-hosted option.** Some GCs can't or won't put financial data in someone else's cloud. Pitbull can run on their own servers.
- **Data flows automatically.** Time entry flows to payroll flows to certified payroll flows to the GL. No CSV exports. No re-keying. No "let me check the other system."

## Objection Handling

**"We already use Procore."**
> Great. How much are you spending on Vista/Sage on top of that? What happens when your PM enters a change order in Procore and your controller has to re-enter it in Vista? That's the problem we solve. One system, one entry, one source of truth.

**"Procore has more integrations."**
> Integrations exist because the product doesn't do the job alone. You need 8 integrations because Procore can't do accounting. We don't need an integration to QuickBooks because we ARE the books.

**"You're too new / too small."**
> We shipped 92 pages, 3,200+ tests, full RBAC, AIA billing, certified payroll, and AI features in weeks, not years. We move faster than Procore's annual release cycle. And we're not asking you to bet the farm. Try the demo, run it parallel for a month, see for yourself.

**"Can you match Procore's field tools?"**
> For daily reports, RFIs, submittals, punch lists, and schedule? Yes. The difference is when your super enters time in the field, it flows straight to payroll and certified payroll without anyone touching it again. Procore can't do that because they don't have payroll.

## Killer Question

> "When your PM approves a change order in Procore, how does that dollar amount get into your accounting system? Who re-enters it, and how long does that take?"

The answer is always: someone manually enters it again in Vista/Sage. That's the gap. That's where money falls through the cracks. That's what we fix.
