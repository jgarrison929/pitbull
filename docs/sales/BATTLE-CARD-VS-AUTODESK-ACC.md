# Battle Card: Pitbull vs Autodesk Construction Cloud (ACC)

## The Short Version

Autodesk bought PlanGrid, BIM 360, and BuildingConnected and stitched them together into "Autodesk Construction Cloud." It covers PM, field, design, and preconstruction. What it doesn't cover: money. No accounting, no payroll, no billing, no job costing.

So you buy ACC for PM and field, then you still need Vista or Sage for financials. Sound familiar? It's the same Procore problem with a bigger price tag and a design focus.

Pitbull covers the full lifecycle. PM + Accounting + Payroll + AI. One platform.

## Where We Win

- **Full lifecycle.** ACC stops at the project boundary. When the work is done and it's time to bill, close the books, and pay people, ACC has nothing. You're back in Vista. Pitbull follows the dollar from bid to final payment.
- **Total cost.** ACC for PM + Vista/Sage for financials = $100-200K/year for a mid-size GC. Pitbull does both for $59K.
- **AI is the architecture, not a feature.** Autodesk bolted an AI chatbot onto their existing tools. Our AI sees project data, financial data, and operational data in one place. It can tell you "this RFI will cost $45K in delays" because it sees the cost codes, the schedule, and the contract.
- **Self-hosted.** Some GCs want their data on their own servers. Autodesk is cloud-only. We give them the choice.
- **Speed of innovation.** Autodesk has 14,000 employees and ships updates quarterly. We ship weekly. Their integration between PlanGrid, BIM 360, and BuildingConnected is still clunky because they're separate acquisitions glued together.

## Where They Win

- **Design and BIM.** This is Autodesk's DNA. Revit, Navisworks, BIM 360. If a GC does heavy design-build or BIM coordination, ACC is hard to beat on the design side. We don't have BIM viewing yet.
- **Document management.** ACC's document control (especially from the PlanGrid side) is mature. Drawing management, revision control, markup tools. Our document management is functional but newer.
- **Preconstruction.** BuildingConnected gives them bid management and sub pre-qualification that's more mature than ours.
- **Enterprise presence.** Autodesk is in every architect's office and most engineers' offices. The construction cloud rides that existing relationship. IT departments trust the Autodesk name.
- **Hardware integrations.** Laser scanning, reality capture, drone integrations. Autodesk has the design-to-field pipeline that we don't touch.

## Objection Handling

**"We need BIM coordination tools."**
> For BIM viewing and clash detection, Autodesk is strong today. But ask yourself: does your accounting team use BIM? Does your payroll department? ACC gives your project team a great tool and leaves everyone else on a different system. We give everyone one platform. And BIM viewing is on our roadmap for later this year.

**"Autodesk is an industry standard."**
> For design tools, absolutely. For construction operations and finance? They bought their way in with PlanGrid and BIM 360, and the integration is still rough. Ask anyone using ACC how smooth the handoff is between BuildingConnected bids and BIM 360 projects. Then ask how their change orders get into their accounting system.

**"ACC has better mobile apps."**
> PlanGrid's mobile app is solid for document viewing and daily reports. But when your super enters time on their phone, where does that data go? Not to payroll. Not to certified payroll. Not to the GL. It stays in ACC's silo. In Pitbull, field data flows to financials automatically.

**"We're already in the Autodesk ecosystem."**
> You can keep Revit for design. We're not replacing your CAD tools. We're replacing the operational and financial stack that ACC doesn't cover. Use Autodesk for design, use Pitbull for everything from groundbreaking to final payment.

## Killer Question

> "When your PM approves a pay app in ACC, how does that billing data get into your general ledger? Who does that work, and how many hours a month does it take?"

It doesn't get there automatically. Someone re-enters it. And for a GC running 10+ projects, that's a full-time job that shouldn't exist.
